# Worldbuilding Documentation

## Core Premise

LingXu presents an immersive framework for understanding China's historical cultivation civilization. The central thesis treats historical records of spiritual practices not as superstition or fantasy, but as fragments of a genuine once-thriving civilization that underwent systemic decline.

### The Central Question

> Why does a once-genuine cultivation civilization leave only fragments in the modern world?

This question drives all content creation and frames how historical information is presented and interpreted.

## The Age of Declining Dharma (末法纪)

### Definition

The Age of Declining Dharma (末法纪) is the central organizing concept of LingXu's worldbuilding. It represents:

1. **Religious Concept**: The Buddhist prediction of佛法衰微 (dharma decline)
2. **Civilizational Model**: The decline of cultivation civilization as a historical process
3. **Analytical Framework**: A lens for understanding why spiritual practices became marginalized

### Three Meanings of "Declining Dharma"

1. **Religious Meaning**: In Buddhism, the prediction that after the Buddha's death,佛法 will pass through stages of正法 (true dharma),像法 (imitative dharma), and末法 (declining dharma).

2. **Civilizational Meaning**: The broader decline of spiritual civilization, including:
   - 灵气衰竭 (spiritual energy depletion)
   - 法脉断裂 (transmission breaks)
   - 神异不显 (spiritual abilities becoming hidden)

3. **LingXu's Framework**: The idea that the current era represents the deep stage of this decline, where only textual and folkloric remnants remain visible.

## The Six Eras (六纪)

### Era Definitions

| Era | Chinese | Period | Characteristics |
|-----|---------|--------|-----------------|
| Primordial | 太初纪 | Cosmic origin | 宇宙肇始，万气肇基 |
| Divine | 神人纪 | Ancient times | 上古真人，乘天地之正 |
| Human Sovereign | 人皇纪 | Three Sovereigns | 三皇五帝，圣王垂范 |
| Dharma Lineage | 法脉纪 | Buddhist-Daoist | 佛教东渐，道教成型 |
| Withdrawal | 隐退纪 | Decline begins | 灵气日衰，真法渐失 |
| Declining Dharma | 末法纪 | Modern era | 灵炁衰竭，唯余典籍 |

### Era Transitions

Transitions between eras are characterized by:
- Gradual decline in spiritual energy
- Loss of transmission lineages
- Increasing obscurity of true methods
- Rise of folk and literary variations

## Remnant Theory

### Categories of Remnants

**Textual Remnants (典籍残痕)**
Historical texts that preserve genuine knowledge:
-《黄帝内经》: Medical and cultivation knowledge
-《山海经》: Geographical and mythological records
-《抱朴子》: Alchemical and practice methods
-《易经》: Cosmological frameworks

**Religious Remnants (宗教残痕)**
Institutional preservation through Buddhism and Daoism:
- Buddhist meditation practices
- Daoist inner alchemy
- Liturgical and ritual traditions
- Monastic transmission systems

**Folk Remnants (民俗残痕)**
Popular practices with cultivation origins:
- Feng shui (风水)
- Fortune-telling (算命)
- Traditional medicine (中医)
- Festival rituals (祭祀)

**Literary Remnants (文学残痕)**
Fictional adaptations and reimagining:
- 志怪小说 (zhiguai tales)
- 仙侠小说 (xianxia novels)
- Oral traditions
- Performing arts

### Interpreting Remnants

Remnants should be analyzed for:
1. Historical kernel (what genuine practice might this reflect?)
2. Cultural context (why did this form persist?)
3. Symbolic meaning (what does this reveal about the original?)
4. Transmission path (how did it survive?)

## Key Concepts

### Spiritual Energy (灵气/灵炁)

The fundamental energy that powers cultivation:
- Pervades the universe
- Can be cultivated and refined
- Subject to environmental conditions
- Declining availability in modern era

### Cultivation (修行)

The systematic practice of spiritual development:
- Multiple paths (丹道, 禅修, 符箓, etc.)
- Defined stages and realms
- Physical and spiritual components
- Transmission-dependent

### Dharma Lineages (法脉)

The chains of transmission for cultivation methods:
- Teacher to student
- Through institutions (宗门, 门派)
- At risk of breaking
- Sometimes preserved in texts

### True Persons (真人)

Those who achieved spiritual completion:
- Inhabited earlier eras
- Demonstrated extraordinary abilities
- Their records form the basis of cultivation theory
- Largely absent in modern times

## Worldbuilding Principles

### Acknowledging Uncertainty

- Not claiming definitive knowledge
- Presenting multiple interpretations
- Acknowledging gaps in the record
- Inviting reader exploration

### Cultural Respect

- Treating historical beliefs seriously
- Avoiding condescension toward historical actors
- Recognizing the value of these traditions
- Preserving cultural heritage

### Narrative Framing

- Using evocative but not sensationalistic language
- Building atmosphere through detail
- Connecting concepts across sections
- Creating a coherent overall picture

### Scholarly Foundation

- Grounding claims in real historical sources
- Distinguishing proven from speculative
- Citing classical texts appropriately
- Acknowledging academic debates
